Broiler Growth Dynamics & Calculus Derivatives

Applying $f'(x)$ to optimize poultry harvest cycles and feeding regimes.

The Biological Problem

In commercial closed-house systems across Malaysia, monitoring daily weight variations helps producers benchmark performance against reference targets. Instead of tracking broad weekly averages, **calculus derivatives** allow us to compute the precise daily speed of mass accumulation.

Mathematical Models

Growth Curve Function:
f(x) = 2800 / (1 + e-0.16(x - 26))

Models cumulative broiler weight (grams) over time $x$ (days).

First Derivative (Growth Rate):
f'(x) = 448 · e-0.16(x - 26) / (1 + e-0.16(x - 26))2

Derived using the quotient/chain rule; calculates weight gain velocity (g/day).
